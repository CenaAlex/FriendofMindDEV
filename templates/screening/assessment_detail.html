What This Assessment Measures

{% if assessment.name == 'phq9' %}

The PHQ-9 (Patient Health Questionnaire-9) is a widely used and validated screening tool for depression. It measures:

  • Mood changes and feelings of sadness
  • Loss of interest or pleasure in activities
  • Sleep disturbances
  • Energy levels and fatigue
  • Appetite and eating changes
  • Concentration difficulties
  • Self-worth and guilt feelings
  • Psychomotor changes
  • Thoughts of self-harm
{% elif assessment.name == 'gad7' %}

The GAD-7 (Generalized Anxiety Disorder 7-item scale) is a reliable tool for screening anxiety disorders. It measures:

  • Feelings of nervousness and anxiety
  • Difficulty controlling worry
  • Excessive worrying about different things
  • Trouble relaxing
  • Restlessness and agitation
  • Irritability
  • Fear that something awful might happen
{% else %}

The Perceived Stress Scale (PSS) measures the degree to which situations in your life are appraised as stressful. It evaluates:

  • How unpredictable your life feels
  • How uncontrollable your life feels
  • How overloaded your life feels
  • Your ability to cope with unexpected events
  • Your confidence in handling personal problems
  • How often things go your way
{% endif %}
